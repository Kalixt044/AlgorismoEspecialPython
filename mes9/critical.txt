ENGLISH
We live immersed in an ocean of media. But are we truly seeing it for what it is?

Have you ever felt the news isn't as neutral as it seems? Or that a movie made you believe something that wasn't real? 
Have you thought an ad was selling you a dream, not just a product?

These questions are the gateway to Media Literacy. It's the skill of reading between the lines in a world full of screens.

First, let's understand what "media" is. It's any channel of communication: from TV and newspapers, to a TikTok video,
a video game, or a podcast. When it reaches a massive audience, it becomes mass media. And each one presents us with a "text"... 
but not just of words. An ad, a song, a meme... they are all texts communicating a message!

To decipher these messages, we need a superpower: Critical Thinking.


It's not about being cynical, it's about being curious. It's about analyzing information with logic and asking: Who created this?
Why? What do they want me to feel or do?

Because, pay attention: you can't always believe what you see. Media use effects, editing, and powerful narratives to construct 
realities. Every image, every sound, is there for a reason. Media Literacy is your antidote to manipulation. It gives you the lens
to see the strategies behind the content.

For example, in advertising...

    Identification: They use people like you, so you feel identified.

    Generalization: They say "everyone has it," to make you feel you're missing out.

    Emotional Appeal: They connect the product with a feeling: happiness, belonging, success.

    Celebrity Endorsement: They use famous people to lend credibility to the product.

In the end, the power is in your hands. Media messages reflect the values of their creators. Your job is to identify them and 
decide: Do I agree?

Take control. Take a second look, a third one. Deconstruct the message.

(More empowering and conclusive tone)

Form your own opinion. Don't just repeat what others want you to believe. Be an active viewer, not a passive spectator.
