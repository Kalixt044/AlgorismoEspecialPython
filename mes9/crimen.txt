Title: Crime and Punishment

I. Introduction and Core Definitions

Welcome. In this audio, we will explore the essential vocabulary of the legal system and illegal activities. Let's start with two 
key concepts.

First, "Crime". A crime is an illegal activity, such as murder or robbery.

And its consequence, "Punishment". A punishment is a penalty imposed on someone convicted of a crime. For example: time in prison 
or a fine.

II. Common Types of Crime

NARRATOR:
Now, let's delve into the types of crime. Listen carefully.

    Abduction or Kidnapping: Taking a person to a secret location using force.

    Armed Robbery: Using a weapon to steal.

    Murder or Homicide: Taking someone's life through violence.

    Burglary, Breaking and Entering: Entering someone else's home or business by force.

    Drug Trafficking: Trafficking illegal drugs.

    Fraud: Lying or cheating for business or monetary purposes.

    Vandalism: Damaging public or private property, for example, with spray paint.

(Brief pause with a subtle sound effect)
III. Common Types of Punishment

NARRATOR:
For every crime, there is a punishment. Let's look at the most common ones.

    Traffic Ticket: For offenses like speeding. It's a fine that marks your driving record.

    License Suspension: For crimes like drunk driving. It means your driving rights are removed for a set period.

    Community Service: For a young person who steals a car for the first time. It involves doing volunteer work.

    Jail Time: For a man who assaults his wife. Spending a certain number of months or years locked away.

    Life in Prison: For a woman who commits homicide. Spending the rest of her life in prison without returning to society.

IV. Practical Application: Reported Speech

NARRATOR (Slightly more conversational tone):
Now, let's practice how to report a crime. We will move from direct speech to reported speech.

Direct speech: "A thief broke into the shop last night and stole some money."
Reported speech: Maria told me that a thief had broken into the shop last night and had stolen some money.

Another example. Direct: "Someone is drunk-driving on the highway."
Reported: A friend told me that someone was drunk-driving along the highway.

V. Conclusion

NARRATOR:
In summary, knowing this "Crime and Punishment" vocabulary is crucial for understanding and discussing news and legal events. 
We encourage you to practice these terms.

II. Tipos Comunes de Crimen

LOCUTOR:

III. Tipos Comunes de Castigo

LOCUTOR:

    Multa de Tráfico: Para ofensas como exceso de velocidad. Es una multa que deja marca en tu historial.

    Servicio Comunitario: Para un joven que roba un coche por primera vez. Es hacer trabajo voluntario.

    Tiempo en Cárcel: Para un hombre que agrede a su esposa. Pasar meses o años encerrado.

    Cadena Perpetua: Para una mujer que comete homicidio. Pasar el resto de la vida en prisión.
